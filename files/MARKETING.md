### Science Credibility Block
**Why PassBoard works when flashcards don't**

Most students study the wrong way — rereading notes until they feel familiar. Familiarity isn't memory. PassBoard's AI study system is engineered using six evidence-based learning techniques used by top medical and licensing exam programs:

1. **Retrieval Practice** — Answering questions forces your brain to actively recall information, building stronger memory than passive reading. *(Roediger & Butler, 2011, Perspectives on Psychological Science)*
2. **Spaced Repetition** — Your personalized study schedule spaces review to catch information right before it fades from memory. *(Ebbinghaus forgetting curve research, applied by Cepeda et al., 2006)*
3. **Elaborative Interrogation** — Every explanation answers "why" — because understanding why something is true makes it 2-3× more memorable than memorizing it. *(Pressley et al., 1992)*
4. **Dual Coding** — Real-world analogies pair verbal explanations with visual mental images, giving your brain two pathways to the same information. *(Paivio, 1991)*
5. **Interleaving** — Questions from different topics are mixed together, matching real PSI exam conditions and preventing "topic tunnel vision." *(Kornell & Bjork, 2008, Psychological Science)*
6. **Metacognitive Monitoring** — Your mastery ratings show you exactly what you know and don't know so you study what matters, not what's comfortable. *(Dunlosky et al., 2013, Psychological Science in the Public Interest)*
